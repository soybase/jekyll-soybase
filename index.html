SoyBase integrates genetic and genomic information to aid soybean breeders and researchers. This instance of the site has been ported to a different framework in order to accommodate the rapidly-growing genomic data available. We anticipate that that this transition will continue through 2025. In the meantime, you can also continue to use "SoyBase classic".

The site is organized as follows. See GENOMICS to access resources and tools such as browsers, GlycineMine, and other visualization tools. See DATA COLLECTIONS to access data sets for download. See TOOLS Analysis and visualization tools for exploring and search data.

Tools
Analysis tools for soybean: BLAST search, Trait terms, GWAS/QTL markers and maps etc.

Maps
Soybean genetic maps, the GlycineMine interface to SoyBase data, and visualization tools for exploring data.

Genomics
Genomic resources for soybean: data sets, genome browsers, pan-gene sets, etc.

Data Collections
View synopses of and links to individual SoyBase Datastore collections.

Projects
Projects associated with or contributed by the soybean research-community.

Community
Community resources for soybean research.

About & Contact
Information about soybean data such as nomenclature, data submission. Also, a form to contact us if you have questions or suggestions!

Help & Tutorials
Videos and tutorials for working with SoyBase, and other videos about soybean.

Funding and Development

SoyBase is funded by the USDA-ARS. SoyBase is developed and hosted by the USDA-ARS SoyBase and Legume Clade Database group at Ames, IA, with development assistance from the National Center for Genome Resources (NCGR) with the help of many other researchers in the soybean community.

Citing SoyBase

We use references to SoyBase in the literature as one way to demonstrate the utility of SoyBase to the research community. Simply placing only a link within the paper (e.g., https://www.soybase.org) is not sufficient, as we cannot easily track these sorts of references. Instead, please include one of the references below, and also include 'SoyBase' at least once in your paper as well as the URL, e.g. ....SoyBase (https://legacy.soybase.org)....

Campbell JD, Cameron CT, Cleary AM, Dash S, Lavelle E, Farmer A, Huang W, Novak S, Prom C, Weeks NT, Cannon SB, Nelson RT. The SoyBase, LegumeInfo and PeanutBase databases in support of legume research and crop improvement GENETICS 2026 232(4). doi: 10.1093/genetics/iyag020. PMID: 41578868

Brown AV, Conners SI, Huang W, Wilkey AP, Grant D, Weeks NT, Cannon SB, Graham MA, Nelson RT. A new decade and new data at SoyBase, the USDA-ARS soybean genetics and genomics database. Nucleic Acids Res. 2021 Jan 8;49(D1):D1496-D1501. doi: 10.1093/nar/gkaa1107. PMID: 33264401; PMCID: PMC7778910.

Grant D, Nelson RT, Cannon SB, Shoemaker RC. SoyBase, the USDA-ARS soybean genetics and genomics database Nucleic Acids Res. 2010 Jan;38(Database issue):D843-6. doi: 10.1093/nar/gkp798. Epub 2009 Dec 14. PMID: 20008513; PMCID: PMC2808871.

Citing Data from the Data Store:

Citations of data sets from the Data Store should reference the original paper that discribes the data. These references can be found in the README file of the in the Data Store collection for the data that you use.
